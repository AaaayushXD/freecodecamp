Dr. Sanduk Ruit

The Man who gave vision to millions of blind people, also known as God of Sight

Timeline of Sanduk Ruit

  • 1954 - Born in the remote mountainous village Olangchunggola in Nepal
  • 1984 - Completed MD in Ophthalmology from the all India Institute of Medical Sciences(AIIMS)
  • 1986 - Ruit and Fred Hollows developed a strategy for using inexpensive intraocular lenses to bring small-incision cataract surgery to the developing world.
  • 1987 -Went to Australia for further Studies
  • 1987 - Married to Nanda Shrestha
  • 1994 - Founded the Tilganga Eye Center, now called the Tilganga Institute of Ophthalmology, in Kathmandu. It provides free treatment for those who are financially disadvantaged. It also produces a high-quality intraocular lens for surgery at a fraction of the cost of its predecessor production.
  • 1995 - Developed RUITECTOMY which, as of 2010, is used in over 60 countries.
  • 2006 - Awarded the Ramon Magsaysay Award for peace and nobel understanding also known as Asia's Nobel Prize.
  • 2007 - Ruit was appointed an Honorary Officer of the Order of Australia
  • 2018 - Government of India honoured him with the Padma Shri, India's fourth-highest civilian honour.
  • 2020 - Nepal Government announced Dr Sanduk Ruit, will be honoured with Suprasiddha Prabal Janasewashree (first).
